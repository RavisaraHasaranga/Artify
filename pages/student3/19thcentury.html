19th Century Art


Neoclassicism was the preeminent painting movement in Europe in 1800, during the beginning of the 19th Century. The decorative movement, which included the ornamental arts, sculpture, and architecture, had evolved in the 18th century, long before the artistic movement.

The emergence of science as a profession and two massive industrial revolutions, which established the time as the age of the machine and had a profound effect on every level of society while enhancing nearly every aspect of daily life, also contributed to the period's enormous social change and urbanization. It was the same with art, particularly painting, in the 19th century. The move from historical "Old Masters" style paintings to the emergence of Modernism over a century was marked by significant changes. Formerly, artists were hired to create works for a patron or an institution; nevertheless, it wasn't until the 19th century that they truly began to create works on their own initiative, pursuing fresh personal interests.

The Apotheosis of Homer by Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres, 1827. The painting shows the ancient writer Homer being crowned by a winged figure representing Victory. Other figures represented in the painting include Dante, Virgil, Raphael and Moliere. Louvre Museum, Paris.
